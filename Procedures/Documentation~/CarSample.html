Car Sample

The car sample project demonstrates how to use Procedures to model a simple car with ignition, engine, steering and lights.

The majority of logic is authored without code, with only a few small scripts to handle player input and the car controller.

The car has the following features:

  • The player can toggle the ignition on/off. When the ignition is on, the engine starts and the car can be driven.
  • The player can toggle the lights on/off independently of the ignition.
  • The player can steer the car left/right
  • The car controller handles acceleration, braking and steering based on player input and the engine state.
  • The engine consumes fuel while running; if fuel runs out, the engine stops and the car can no longer be driven until refueled.

Components Overview:

  • Ignition: A simple Procedure, synchronizing its state with the Engine using a LinkedProcedure.
  • Engine: A Procedure with constraints (ignition on, fuel available) and outputs audio
  • Lights: A Procedure linked to a ProcedureBehaviour, which toggles the light GameObjects on start/stop.
  • Fuel: A variable authored in the VariableStore, consumed by the Engine procedure.
  • Car Steering: A simple Monobehaviour that reads player input and sets the steering angle on the car controller.
  • Car Control: A simple Monobehaviour that handles acceleration, braking and steering based on player input and the Engine state.
