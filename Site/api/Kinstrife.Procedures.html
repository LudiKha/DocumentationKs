Activators

The Activators module enables a procedure to be activated or deactivated based on specific conditions or triggers defined in its instructions. It contains a list of IActivatorInstruction instructions that specify the criteria for activation, such as resource levels, time-based triggers, or external events and messages. Common use cases are registering to stat changes (health/stamina), time of day messages or application flow events to activate the procedure.

Outputs

Outputs represent actions or effects that occur as a result of a procedure's lifecycle events, such as starting, stopping, or continuing. It contains a list of IOutputInstruction instructions that define specific outputs to be executed based on the procedure's operations.(Operation) Common examples of outputs include playing sounds, triggering animations, sending notifications, or activating visual effects. More complex outputs can involve consuming variables, interacting with other systems, or executing sequences of actions.

Procedure

A Procedure is a modular, data-driven behaviour that can be started and stopped, and that manages its own state and side-effects via reusable components. They are ScriptableObjects that can be authored as assets and instantiated at runtime. They are composed from reusable ProcedureComponents ProcedureComponent that provide specific functionality, such as handling outputs, evaluating constraints, or executing tasks. Procedures can be started and stopped based on internal logic or external requests, and they manage their own lifecycle events.
